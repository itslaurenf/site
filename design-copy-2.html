DESIGN THINKING

  • Design thinking is all about empathetic, iterative, cross-disciplinary problem solving - and anticipating the problems we might not notice. (...)

    Once you understand the problem and its stakeholders, design thinking really unfolds into an iterative process of exploring and refining different possible solutions, from concept ideation to testing prototypes (over and over again). Whether you're designing a service, product, or experience, the goal is to create something that'll improve the world.

     

    This design thinking mindset has been a huge part of my time at Northwestern, and I've found it applicable across all my studies, in understanding what makes people feel to best create an experience for them as well as in bringing the creative and the technical together.

Compass

Service Design, Spring 2014. Career mentoring program (comparable to freshman orientation groups) that seeks to reduce career-related stress on campus by providing an accessible, relatable means of support by students, for students. Challenge: to improve mental/emotional health at Northwestern.

Floormate

App Design, Winter 2015. An app to help college students alleviate awkward noise confrontation in dorms by indicating noise preference “status” anonymously. Pitched to Residential Board as an effort to improve dorm life; Res Board is currently considering making this a reality.

Pitch Slides // Handout // Prototype (PoP)

Stride

Industrial Design, Fall 2014. A sleek, portable cane-walker to improve senior mobility for the handicapped aboard a domestic flight. We were tasked with designing a brand-consistent product for a randomly chosen company (Virgin Airlines) and demographic (men 65-70).

Pitch Slides // Process Work

Defrosty

Product Design, Fall 2015. Frozen meat? Snow problem! Defrosty is a novel “bag in a bag” meat defrosting solution that helps you defrost meat quickly and sanitarily while maintaining its quality. After iterations of competitive benchmarking and prototyping, we ended up filing a provisionary patent.
